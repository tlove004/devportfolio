Engineering Experience

University of California, Riverside (UCR)

Computer Science Researcher

  • Specified, designed, and implemented MediSyn, a modular pharmaceutical discovery and synthesis framework which provides researchers efficient stochastic exploration and verification of candidate pharmaceuticals via Markov chain Monte Carlo simulation. Candidate spaces – represented by probabilistic context-free grammars – are synthesized through the application of state-of-the art methods in machine learning.
  • Collaborated on the design, specification, and implementation BioScript, a domain-specific-language and chemical typesystem (ChemType) enabling biologists and chemists to express experiments for automated design and safe execution, and a utilization of ChemType as ChemStor, a chemical storage and disposal system that provides sound safety guarantees through the use of the z3 SMT solver.
  • Defined the time- and resource-constrained scheduling problem for programmable laboratories-on-a-chip, which enforces fine-tuned timing constraints on timing-sensitive chemical interactions specified in a high level language for resource-constrained reconfigurable architectures lacking external storage; additionally, an optimal ILP formulation and an efficient heuristic scheduler were implemented to solve this problem.
  • Developed a NSGA-II-based placement algorithm for digital microfluidic biochip synthesis, which enables the placement of operations in complex control-directed chemical programs to be optimized across basic block boundaries through a guided stochastic search.
  • Designed and developed a performance-optimizing compiler for cyber-physical digital microfluidic biochips which reduces or eliminates droplet routing requirements through a coalescing mechanism on an interference graph and reduces overall program latency by exploring the complex relation between operation-level parallelism and reconfigurable register sizes.
  • Managed a team of 10-15 computer science undergraduate and graduate researchers, teaching them best practices for writing production-quality code through code reviews, writing test cases, and specifying direction to further research endeavors.
